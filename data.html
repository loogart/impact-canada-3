Data & Integration

Overview

  • Why Data and Measurement Integration?

    Connecting data, measurement, and reporting to help tell the story of departmental progress ― In today's digital landscape, organizations gather vast quantities of data from a wide range of sources, including surveys, administrative forms, apps, spreadsheets, cloud services and APIs, among many others. In most cases, data are stored in different formats and locations with varying levels of quality or completeness, leading to data silos, misalignments, and inconsistencies. All these affect departments’ abilities to make well-informed decisions, monitor- and report on results, and assess effectiveness of decisions.

    Data and measurement integration involves a series of steps and a combination of technical processes, tools, and expertise to create strategies that bring together data from disparate sources, transforming it into a unified and consistent structure that makes it more accessible and more usable for meaningful analysis and decision-making.

  • What can Impact Canada do for partner departments?

    Leveraging our expertise in the development of data strategies, data collection and management, data science, model use, and the incorporation of data visualization products into decision-making and impact evaluation, the Impact and Innovation Unit (IIU) supports partner departments to:

    1. Advance data and measurement integration to better inform the design, implementation, and evaluation of priority programs, services, and initiatives across Canada and to meet key departmental objectives. 
    2. Diversify the collection, management, linkage, and use of data through a framework approach to support reporting, improve the evidence base for decision-making, and more effectively target federal investments.
    3. Undertake data process sludge audits to evaluate the efficiency of processes for capturing and preparing data, or the efficiency of data use for analysis processes to optimize investments and reduce costs.
    4. Review legacy systems or long-standing processes to support renewal, discover inconsistencies and duplication that develop over time, and support transition to modern, open, reproducible data science practices.

  • Our approach: The data and measurement integration process

    Impact Canada's Data and Measurement Integration team uses a system-level lens and collaborative, four-phase process to unearth hidden challenges and duplication, disentangle complex processes into their constituent parts, and address key misalignments across an organization’s data, measurement, and reporting ecosystem.

    1 - Discover – Scoping and Initial Problem Identification

    The Discover phase uses technical workshops, document reviews, interviews, and in-depth data and information gap analyses to understand and document the current state of a department’s data ecosystem – including its data holdings and acquisition, storage, management, and data use practices, and linkages and interdependencies to internal and external data.

    2 - Ideate  - Designing novel solutions

    The Ideate phase supports departments as they decide on priority paths and seeks to establish agreement on key steps and milestones. This can include:

    • Co-developing frameworks and strategies for increased system coherence and improved data use
    • Identifying and leveraging new and non-traditional data sources and measurement strategies
    • Exploring and implementing novel modelling approaches and methods for communicating the information in large data sets
    • Initiating renewal paths to reduce duplication and re-align long standing processes with current reporting requirements
    • Developing strategies to increase willingness to share data by building trust, reducing response burden, or addressing privacy concerns using a behavioural science lens.

    3 - Iterate – Implementing and testing novel solutions

    The Iterate phase is where the IIU and partner departments undertake novel projects. This may include:

    • Providing resources to “jump start” a change
    • Designing and supporting implementation plans
    • Undertaking experimental projects to inform on their costs / benefits
    • Recruiting non-traditional actors and partners to provide feedback

    4 - Transfer- Building partner capacity to maintain and extend investments with Impact Canada

    A key priority for Impact Canada is supporting capacity growth with partner departments. The Transition phase is where the Impact Canada and partner departments coordinate closely to ensure a smooth transition of knowledge and capabilities so that work extends beyond the end of the partnership.

Overview

  • How we work

    The Data and Measurement Integration Team is innovation-focused, flexible, and responsive. We bring multi-disciplinary expertise and highly specialized support to improve information flows and support advancing data and measurement priorities in partnership with departments.

    We emphasize shared project governance and rapid decision-making, with open collaboration and communication. We start small, focusing on design and proof of concept in our projects. The Data and Measurement Integration Team offers a variety of supports and services to our GC partners including:

    • Reviewing and aligning reporting and data needs
    • Framework development with proposed implementation paths
    • Identification of cost saving strategies and methods to re-purpose existing processes / resources
    • Fitness for use evaluations and suggested methods to better align data with its uses
    • Data pipeline / analysis process sludge audits to improve efficiency and save on time and costs
    • Book a meeting and learn more about how we can tailor our services to your organization.

  • Expert guidance

    If our GC partners need to integrate data and measurement insights into the design of a new initiative, new policy or new program on a short time horizon, we can offer recommendations and guidance on the basis of rapid, relevant document reviews and interviews, and our expertise and experience.
