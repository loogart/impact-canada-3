Fellowship Program

The Fellowship Program recruits unique expertise to help support departments and agencies in addressing the government's top priorities. Through skilled recruitment, expert guidance, and a tailored approach, Fellows have opportunities to make tangible contributions to public service initiatives.

Overview

  • What is the Fellowship Program?

    Impact Canada launched the Fellowship program to attract external talent with specialized skills and expertise. The program supports departments and agencies by building capacity to apply innovative, outcomes-based financing approaches, new partnership models, impact measurement methodologies, and social science insights to priority areas.

    Connecting talent with Government needs.

    The Fellowship Program bridges expertise gaps in areas such as research, data analysis, and policy design. By recruiting specialists from around the world, the program provides departments with the expertise needed to accelerate priority initiatives. Fellows bring fresh perspectives, and their contributions lead to demonstrable results across Canada.

  • The right skills at the right time

    Impact Canada’s model is designed to complement partner departments’ existing expertise in a manner that maximizes efficiency of resources. Together, Impact Canada’s Centre of Expertise and host organizations establish goals and determine project scope and approach. Once a Memoranda of Understanding (MoU) is in place, the Centre of Expertise helps guide projects,  leveraging the Fellowship program to ensure the teams have the right skills at the right time to achieve success. 

    Fellows’ placements are tailored to match their strengths and operational needs of the project.

  • In-house expertise

    Fellows are typically embedded in host departments, where they receive the support, tools, workspace, and resources required to succeed in their roles. Departmental partners assume day-to-day oversight of embedded Fellows’ work and provide leadership and direction to them. Fellows work alongside the Impact Canada Centre of Expertise. This direct connection is maintained to supplement the guidance provided to Fellows, leverage internal and centrally positioned expertise, and enhance  research and translation of findings into  actionable policy, program and regulatory recommendations. 

  • Creating new opportunities across the country

    Impact Canada Fellows are recruited as term employees by the Privy Council Office (PCO), with technical expertise in their respective domains. Fellows serve as skilled specialists in research design, methodologies, data analysis, impact measurement, and knowledge translation, contributing to projects that accelerate progress in priority areas. We recruit Fellows from around the world, with applicants from as far as India, Japan, and New Zealand, creating opportunities for Canadian Citizens, permanent residents, and work visa/permit holders.

    Fellows are located across the country, not limited to the National Capital Region, coming predominately from outside the traditional pathways to Government of Canada roles, bringing in fresh perspectives and expertise to the public service. 

  • Maximizing efficiencies

    Impact Canada's team implements the Fellowship program, including running efficient and robust recruitment campaigns, assessing candidates on technical skills. We aim for a 90-day target for our recruitment campaigns, from launch to establishing a pool of pre-qualified candidates. To date, we have completed 12 campaigns, with an average campaign length of 77 days. Providing this resourcing solution centrally eliminates the need for individual departments to run multiple [and overlapping] recruitment campaigns, creating a more efficient and streamlined process.

    Impact Canada Fellows receive comprehensive onboarding, providing them with tools and connections for early and ongoing success, tailored to their host department placement. Onboarding is designed to define and achieve goals from the perspectives of Fellows, host managers, and home managers, integrating the new Fellow into the program’s broad and interdisciplinary cohort.

  • Eligibility

    The Fellowship program employs both Canadian Citizens and Permanent Residents. Canadian Citizens residing in Canada or abroad, as well as Permanent Residents residing in Canada, are eligible to apply. As per the Public Service Employment Act, preference will be given to a person who is in receipt of a pension by reason of war service; a veteran or a survivor of a veteran; and a Canadian citizen or permanent resident, in that order.

    As per the Qualification standards for the core public administration by occupational group or classification, eligible applicants must possess a degree obtained from a recognized post-secondary institution.

    The Fellowship program evaluates candidates based on technical skills rather than years of experience. Individuals from all employment backgrounds are eligible to apply.

    The Public Service of Canada is committed to building a skilled and diverse workforce that reflects the Canadians we serve. The Fellowship program is committed to upholding employment equity and diversity and encourages you to indicate if you belong to one of the designated groups when you apply. Information on employment equity.

    The Fellowship program employs unilingual English, unilingual French, and bilingual candidates.

  • The 90-day process

    Our recruitment processes are uniquely designed to pre-qualify individuals based on technical expertise rather than years of experience. This allows us to identify individuals who bring unique skills and fresh perspectives to the public service.

    We are committed to creating equal opportunities for Canadian citizens and permanent residents and integrate equity and diversity considerations in our recruitment process. Our approach includes diversified outreach, promotional activities, and inclusive evaluation criteria, ensuring fair and representative selection.

    • Day 1

      Recruitment campaign launch

    • 1 Application

      Applicants submit their responses to a tailored questionnaire designed to assess technical skills.

    • 2 Written exam

      Successful applicants complete a written exam designed to assess their ability to apply their technical skills.

    • 3 Interview

      Successful applicants are invited to an interview to further asses their technical skills, with a focus on their personal suitability (soft skills), to help identify the right fit for the Fellowship program.

    • 4 References and security

      Successful applicants advance to references and the security verification process is undertaken.

    • 5 Pre-qualification

      Applicants having successfully advanced through steps 01-04 are eligible for placement upon issuance of their security clearance.

    • Day 90

      Completion of recruitment process.
